## Line Plot vs Scatter Plot â€” When to Use Each

### Key Difference (One Sentence)

- **Scatter plots** show the *relationship* between two variables.
- **Line plots** show how a variable *changes over an ordered axis* (usually time).

---
### Scatter Plot

**Purpose:** Examine association between two variables  
**Order matters?**  No  
**Continuity implied?**  No

**Best for:**

- Correlation (positive / negative / none)
- Clusters
- Outliers
- Nonlinear relationships

**Typical examples:**

- Height vs weight
- Price vs demand
- Study hours vs exam score

**Question answered:**
> â€œAre these two variables related?â€

---
### Line Plot

**Purpose:** Show change over an ordered variable  
**Order matters?**  Yes  
**Continuity implied?**  Yes

**Best for:**

- Trends over time
- Growth or decline
- Seasonality
- Rates of change

**Typical examples:**

- Daily sales over time
- Temperature by hour
- Stock prices

**Question answered:**
> â€œHow does this variable evolve?â€

---
### Why Both Show â€œRelationshipsâ€

- Both plot **y vs x**
- Difference is **interpretation**, not mechanics

| Plot Type | Relationship Meaning             |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| Scatter   | Statistical association          |
| Line      | Sequential / temporal dependence |

---
### Simple Decision Rule
> **If you shuffle the x-axis and the plot still makes sense â†’ Scatter plot**  
> **If it stops making sense â†’ Line plot**

---

### Important Note
- **Line plots imply interpolation between points**
- **Scatter plots do not**

Use scatter for independent observations,  
use line plots when continuity matters.
